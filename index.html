The Battle of Stalingrad

17 July 1942 - 2 February 1943


The battle of Stalingrad was the brutal, urban battle that was the turning point of the second World War.

After the success of Operation Barbarossa, the Germans had advanced deep into the soviet union. And they were set on their next target: the city of Stalingrad on the Volga river. Capturing this city would mean a great strategic and propagandistic victory for the Wermacht. And on the 17th of July, 1942 the Germans attacked the city.

Account of the battle

The battle was long and brutal, involving hundereds of thousands of troops and hundereds of tanks. The battle was characterized by the intense urban warfare and close combat. The Luftwaffe comensed bombings on the city, bombing civilians.
